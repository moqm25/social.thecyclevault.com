# Product

## Register

product

> The app surface (feed, circles, post views, dashboards) is **product** and carries this default.
> The public entry — `/` landing/welcome — is a **brand** surface and is designed in that register.

## Users

Women and people who menstruate, of all ages and stages (first periods, TTC, PCOS,
endometriosis, perimenopause). They arrive in a vulnerable, often anxious headspace,
looking for honest answers and to feel less alone — frequently on a phone, late at
night. Many are privacy-conscious and wary of being tracked or judged. A smaller set
are verified clinicians/educators offering gentle, evidence-based help, plus
volunteer moderators and admins keeping the space safe.

## Product Purpose

A calmer, privacy-first community for cycle and women's-health conversations — like a
forum, but built to feel safe instead of extractive. Companion to The CycleVault iOS
app (a local-only period tracker). It exists so people can ask, share, and learn
without surveillance, rage-bait, or infinite-scroll mechanics. Success = a person
leaves feeling heard, informed, and calmer than when they arrived, and trusts the
space enough to come back.

## Brand Personality

Calm, kind, trustworthy. Three words: **gentle, private, grounded.** The voice is a
warm, plain-spoken friend who happens to know the science — never clinical-cold,
never chirpy-influencer, never alarmist. It should feel like a quiet, well-lit room,
not a feed competing for attention.

## Anti-references

- **Reddit / generic forums:** combative, anonymous-but-exposed, doom-scroll, karma
  theater, visible pile-ons. We deliberately blur names from logged-out guests.
- **Engagement-optimized social (X, TikTok, IG):** infinite scroll, dopamine loops,
  outrage surfacing, ads that follow you. None of it.
- **Clinical/medical portals:** cold, sterile, fear-inducing, jargon walls.
- **AI-slop SaaS landing pages:** purple mesh-gradient hero, three identical feature
  cards, eyebrow label above every section, fake dashboard screenshots.
- **Femtech cliché:** hot-pink "girl" branding, stock photos of women laughing at
  salad, period euphemisms.

## Design Principles

1. **Privacy is the product, not a feature.** The interface should make safety
   *visible* (blurred guest names, "your data stays yours" said plainly, no tracking).
2. **Calm by design.** No infinite-scroll traps, no red-badge anxiety, no rage-bait
   surfacing. Generous space, soft motion, quiet color. The screen should lower the
   user's heart rate, not raise it.
3. **Welcome before firehose.** A newcomer meets a warm front door and chooses to
   step in — they don't get dropped into a stranger's argument mid-thread.
4. **Earn trust like a clinic's waiting room, delight like a good app.** Familiar,
   legible, dependable product patterns; warmth and personality in the moments that
   matter (empty states, welcomes, milestones).
5. **One continuous brand.** Visually continuous with the iOS app and marketing site
   (coral + lavender, Inter + Fraunces italic accent). Identity preservation wins
   over reinvention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast, 15px minimum body size, visible focus
  rings, full keyboard operability.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` honored globally (calm crossfades / instant, never essential
  motion).
- Light and dark themes, both first-class.
- Inclusive, non-judgmental language; never assumes a body, a goal, or a diagnosis.
- Crisis content handled with care, not punishment; support resources surfaced.
